Emergency Surgery

When to Seek Emergency Care

Immediate Emergency Signs
Difficulty Breathing

Rapid breathing, open-mouth breathing, or blue gums

Severe Bleeding

Uncontrolled bleeding or blood in urine/stool

Collapse or Unconsciousness

Pet unable to stand or unresponsive

Seizures

Multiple seizures or seizures lasting more than 3 minutes

Urgent Care Signs
Vomiting or Diarrhea

Severe, persistent, or bloody vomiting/diarrhea

Lethargy

Extreme tiredness or unwillingness to move

Loss of Appetite

Not eating for more than 24 hours

Pain or Discomfort

Whining, crying, or unusual behavior indicating pain

What to Do in an Emergency

1. Call Us

Call our emergency line immediately: (410) 555-9999

2. Stay Calm

Keep your pet calm and comfortable while preparing to transport

3. Transport Safely

Use a carrier or secure your pet safely for transportation

4. Get Treatment

Our emergency team will provide immediate care upon arrival
